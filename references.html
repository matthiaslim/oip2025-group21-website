Secondary Research: Preexisting Solutions

Comprehensive analysis of existing accessibility solutions across cultural institutions

Accessibility in Attractions

British Museum

Audio + Visual

Multimedia guide system with comprehensive accessibility features including audio descriptions, BSL interpretation, and tactile elements

  • • Multi-language audio tours with detailed descriptions
  • • Audio description for visually impaired visitors
  • • BSL interpreted events and video content
  • • Interactive multimedia content and mobile app integration
Visit British Museum Accessibility

Bloomberg Connects

Mobile Platform

Free digital guide platform used by major museums and cultural institutions worldwide with comprehensive accessibility features

  • • Audio descriptions and guided tours with accessibility options
  • • Screen reader compatibility and high-contrast visual interfaces
  • • Multi-language support and customizable text sizes
  • • Interactive maps with accessibility route planning
Visit Bloomberg Connects

U.S. Botanic Garden (USBG)

Tactile + Sensory

Comprehensive sensory accessibility program with specialized equipment, sensory-friendly programming, and tactile resources

  • • Sensory bags with noise-reducing headphones and weighted lap blankets
  • • Color blind glasses available for enhanced visual experience
  • • Sensory-friendly early access programs for cognitive and sensory disabilities
  • • Mobile-accessible audio tours with detailed plant descriptions
Visit U.S. Botanic Garden Accessibility

Eden Project

BSL + Visual

Award-winning accessibility program with comprehensive visual impairment support and mobility services for botanical experiences

  • • Braille guidebooks and text readers for interpreting signs and information
  • • Pre-bookable Access Guides to support individual visitor needs
  • • ADI toilets specifically designed for blind and visually impaired visitors
  • • Comprehensive mobility support including wheelchairs and electric scooters
Visit Eden Project Accessibility

Key Insights from Preexisting Solutions

Common Success Factors:
  • Multi-modal accessibility: Successful institutions combine audio, visual, tactile, and digital elements for comprehensive access
  • Specialized sensory support: Dedicated equipment like sensory bags, color blind glasses, and text readers enhance visitor experiences
  • Scalable digital platforms: Solutions like Bloomberg Connects demonstrate how technology can provide consistent accessibility across multiple institutions
Applicable to Glasgow Botanic Gardens:
  • Sensory-friendly programming and specialized equipment (USBG model) directly applicable to botanical settings
  • Visual impairment support through Braille guides and text readers (Eden Project approach) proven effective in garden environments
  • Digital audio tour platforms with accessibility features offer cost-effective, scalable solutions for botanical education
