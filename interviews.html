Stakeholder Interview

Understanding current operations and accessibility challenges

Interviewee Details

Name: Chrissy Sanachan
Role: Representative, Friends of Glasgow Botanic Gardens
Date: 22 July 2025
Format: Face-to-face interview
Duration: 45 minutes

Interview Objectives

  • Understand current tour operations and volunteer structure
  • Identify existing accessibility challenges and constraints
  • Explore volunteer capabilities and resource limitations
  • Gather insights on visitor demographics and needs

Inclusion Philosophy

  • • Want to be inclusive to everybody (free and public space)
  • • Been approached by groups with disabilities asking for tours
  • • Had 1 previous disability tour (limited experience)
  • • Strong desire to serve the community better

Insight: Clear mission for inclusivity but lack practical experience and resources

Current Gaps

  • • No measures currently in place for blind and deaf visitors
  • • Local volunteers help but not specifically trained
  • • No reservations/booking system for tours
  • • Limited operations during winter months

Challenge: Complete absence of accessibility infrastructure

Budget Constraints

  • • Low-cost solution preferred
  • • Could offer sensory tours 1-2 times per year
  • • No firm deadline for implementation
  • • Need sustainable, affordable approach

Opportunity: Flexible timeline allows for phased implementation

Kibble Palace - Prime Location for Accessibility

Why Kibble Palace is Ideal:
  • Collection stability: Plants do not change throughout the years
  • Tactile opportunities: Visitors able to touch and feel plants
  • Iconic status: Highlight of the Gardens experience
Accessibility Potential:
  • Consistent content for audio guide development
  • Safe tactile exploration environment
  • Indoor location suitable for year-round access

Key Insights from Interview

"Want to be inclusive to everybody, because it is free and public. Been approached by groups with disabilities if there are such tours available"

— Chrissy Sanachan, Friends of Glasgow Botanic Gardens
Main Concerns Raised:
  • • No current measures for blind and deaf visitors
  • • Budget limitations for new equipment
  • • Volunteers not specifically trained for accessibility
  • • Need for low-cost, sustainable solutions
Expressed Needs:
  • • Simple, practical solutions
  • • Low-cost implementation (publicly funded)
  • • Seasonal flexibility (1-2 times per year)
  • • No firm deadline pressure

Volunteer Guides Interview

Mapping sensory experiences and technology solutions

Interviewee Details

Name: Volunteer Tour Guides
Role: Volunteer guides, Glasgow Botanic Gardens
Date: 31 July 2025
Format: Street Interview
Duration: 1 hour

Interview Objectives

  • Identify key garden locations and highlights for visitors
  • Map sensory experiences throughout the gardens
  • Understand accessibility requirements for different visitor needs
  • Gather insights on technology solutions for accessibility

Key Garden Locations

  • • Kibble Palace: Highlight of the Gardens experience
  • • Glasshouses: "Magical" climate transitions
  • • Herb Garden: Rich sensory experience with diverse scents
  • • Multiple accessible routes and locations

Insight: Identified prime locations for multi-sensory accessibility experiences

Sensory Mapping

  • • Scents: Lavender, mint, rosemary in herb garden
  • • Textures: Various plant surfaces for tactile exploration
  • • Sounds: Natural acoustics in different environments
  • • Climate: Temperature and humidity variations

Opportunity: Rich multi-sensory content available for accessibility design

Technology Solutions

  • • QR codes described as "elegant, modern" solution
  • • Need for reliable Wi-Fi infrastructure
  • • Mobile-optimized interface requirements
  • • Professional BSL video content needed

Requirement: Technology-enabled accessibility solutions preferred by guides

Key Insights from Volunteer Guides

"QR codes linking to BSL videos - that's elegant, modern. For blind visitors, the content needs to be vivid, narrative-rich with texture, shape, smell, and story."

— Volunteer Guides, Glasgow Botanic Gardens
Technology Requirements Identified:
  • • Good Wi-Fi connectivity throughout gardens
  • • Professional BSL signers for video content
  • • Strategic QR code placement at key locations
  • • Mobile-optimized user interface design
Content Specifications:
  • • Vivid, narrative-rich audio descriptions
  • • Detailed texture and shape information
  • • Comprehensive scent mapping and descriptions
  • • Historical context and plant stories

Scent Zones & Sensory Experiences

Tropical Glasshouse

Scent Profile:

Warm, damp earth

Sensory Features:
  • • High humidity and warmth
  • • Rich, earthy aromas
  • • Lush vegetation sounds
  • • Tropical plant textures

Kibble Palace

Scent Profile:

Clean, forest after rain

Sensory Features:
  • • Fresh, clean air quality
  • • Victorian architectural sounds
  • • Mixed plant collection
  • • Natural acoustics

Herb Garden

Scent Profile:

Lavender, mint, rosemary

Sensory Features:
  • • Intense herbal aromas
  • • Tactile leaf textures
  • • Diverse scent combinations
  • • Seasonal variations

Deaf Visitors (BSL Tour)

Technology Solution:

QR codes linking to BSL videos described as "Elegant, modern"

Infrastructure Needs:
  • • Good Wi-Fi: Reliable internet connectivity throughout gardens
  • • Professional signers: Quality BSL video content
  • • QR code placement: Strategic positioning at key locations
  • • Mobile optimization: User-friendly interface design

Blind Visitors (Audio Guide)

Content Requirements:

Needs "vivid, narrative-rich" descriptions

Essential Elements to Include:
  • • Texture: Detailed surface and material descriptions
  • • Shape: Comprehensive spatial and form information
  • • Smell: Rich scent descriptions (as mapped above)
  • • Story: Historical context and plant narratives

Research Summary & Design Implications

What We Confirmed

  • Strong demand for accessibility tours from disabled visitors
  • Kibble Palace offers the richest multi-sensory experience potential
  • QR code technology is preferred by staff and guides
  • Budget constraints require low-cost, sustainable solutions

Design Requirements Identified

  • Audio content: Vivid, narrative-rich descriptions with texture, smell, and story
  • BSL videos: Professional sign language interpretation
  • Technology: Mobile-optimized QR code system
  • Infrastructure: Reliable Wi-Fi throughout gardens

Key Insight for Our Solution

"The interviews revealed that accessibility is not just about compliance—it is about creating rich, engaging experiences that allow all visitors to connect with the Gardens' beauty through multiple senses. Our solution must be both technologically elegant and experientially meaningful."
