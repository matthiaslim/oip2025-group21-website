The Problem Statement

The volunteer guides at Glasgow Botanic Gardens want to enhance their tours to be more meaningful and enjoyable for deaf and/or blind visitors. The core challenge is to understand the unique accessibility needs of these groups and adapt existing tour materials to better serve them, ensuring an inclusive experience for all.

How might we...

Help volunteer guides at the Glasgow Botanic Gardens offer meaningful and accessible tour experiences for blind and/or deaf visitors within the constraints of a public, low-cost environment?

Our Design Process

We adopted the Double Diamond model to guide our design journey.

1

Discover

We conducted an informal, face-to-face interview with our partner and researched existing solutions in other public attractions.

2

Define

We synthesized our research into key insights and formulated our "How Might We" statement to focus our efforts.

3

Develop

We brainstormed diverse ideas and created low-fidelity prototypes (sketches) to explore potential solutions.

4

Deliver

This final stage involves refining our proposed solution based on feedback and developing a clear implementation plan.

Our Proposed Solution

A low-cost, multi-faceted approach to enhance the tour experience.

For Deaf Visitors

Placing QR codes at key points of interest that link to a webpage with visual descriptions and British Sign Language (BSL) video interpretations.

For Blind Visitors

Developing a descriptive audio guide, accessible via their own devices, that includes rich descriptions, ambient sounds, and music to create a sensory experience.

Justification

This solution is suitable because it directly addresses the project's constraints. It is low-cost, as it leverages visitors' own smartphones and avoids expensive hardware. It is sustainable for volunteers, as the digital content can be easily managed and updated. Most importantly, it is inclusive by providing distinct, tailored experiences rather than a one-size-fits-all approach.

Key Actionable Insights

Learnings that will shape our future approach to design challenges

Design Process Learning

  • Prioritize user feedback over assumptions
  • Use prototyping for discovery, not just presentation
  • Test ideas early and iterate quickly

Collaboration & Stakeholders

  • Engage partners throughout the process
  • Consider constraints as design opportunities
  • Focus on sustainable, low-cost solutions

Looking Forward: These insights will guide future accessibility projects and inform our approach to inclusive design challenges, ensuring we maintain a user-centered, collaborative approach to problem-solving.
